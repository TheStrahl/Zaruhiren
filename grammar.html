Zaruhiren Grammar

Alphabet & Pronunciation

No C, Q, or X. Vowels: A (ah), E (eh), I (ee), O (oh), U (oo)—Japanese-style. Consonants: B, D, F, G, H, J, K, L, M, N, P, R, S, T, V, W, Y, Z.

Syntax

Subject-Verb-Object (SVO): "Jiro hunts food" = "Jiro I’susife vadis."

Verbs

Prefixes attach pronouns to verbs with an apostrophe:

  • Te’ - They (singular)
  • Tu’ - They (plural)
  • Mo’ - They (unknown)
  • I’ - I/Myself
  • O’ - You
  • Ya’ - We/Us
  • Ke’ - It/They
  • Hu’ - Who
  • Ha’ - What
  • He’ - When
  • Here’ - Where
  • Hi’ - Why
  • Ho’ - How
  • Hic’ - Which
  • Ne’ - Nobody

Suffixes for tense/modality (e.g., I’crimba = I climb):

  • na - Don’t
  • in - Doing
  • et - Did
  • wa - Will
  • ka - Can
  • ta - Want

Adjectives

Hyphenated to nouns: "red-sand" = "ruba-zaru."

Prepositions

Standalone before nouns: "in house" = "ni poir."

Detail Markers

Control sentence flow: "You called who me" = "O’yoaneet tem I."

Numbers

Base-12: 1 = Una, 12 = Doza, 13 = Doza-una.

Prepositions and Detail Markers

Prepositions (e.g., "fa" = over) come after detail markers (e.g., "hei" = what) and before the noun they modify. Word order: Verb + Detail Marker + Preposition + Noun Phrase.

  • Example: "I jumped over a blue cow" = "I’judikeet hei fa bizu-bovi."
